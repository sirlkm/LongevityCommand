================================================================================
  CARDIAC RISK ENGINE — MODEL PERFORMANCE SUMMARY
================================================================================

MODEL ARCHITECTURE
------------------
Algorithm:          XGBoost (Gradient Boosted Trees)
Trees:              300
Max depth:          5
Learning rate:      0.08
Class balancing:    Automatic (scale_pos_weight)
Feature scaling:    StandardScaler

PERFORMANCE METRICS (80/20 stratified split)
---------------------------------------------
Accuracy:           91.5%
ROC-AUC:            0.82
Precision:          23.5%
Recall:             16.0%

Note on precision/recall: The 6.3% event rate creates class imbalance.
The model optimizes for discrimination (AUC) over raw precision — correctly
ranking patients by risk is more valuable than binary classification.

TRAINING DATA
-------------
Records:            2,000 synthetic patients
Event rate:         6.3% (126 cardiac events)
Generation:         Logistic model with realistic clinical correlations
Seed:               42 (reproducible)

INPUT FEATURES (7 raw + 3 engineered = 10 total)
--------------------------------------------------
Raw features:
  1. age              30–60 years
  2. bmi              20–38 kg/m2
  3. resting_hr       50–95 bpm
  4. hrv_rmssd        20–90 ms
  5. sleep_hours      4–9 hours
  6. stress_score     1–10 scale
  7. recovery_hours   12–48 hours

Engineered features:
  8. cardiovascular_stress  = (resting_hr x stress_score) / hrv_rmssd
  9. recovery_risk          = recovery_hours / sleep_hours
 10. metabolic_load         = bmi x (11 - stress_score) / 10

PREDICTION OUTPUT
-----------------
- Risk score:         0–100 (percentage)
- Risk level:         LOW (<20) / MODERATE (20–49) / HIGH (50+)
- 6-month timeline:   Month-by-month risk trajectory
- Risk factors:       Identified concerning metrics
- Recommendation:     Actionable clinical guidance

KEY INNOVATION
--------------
Individual risk trajectories, not just binary classification.
The timeline shows how a patient's risk compounds over 6 months,
enabling early intervention before events occur.

EXAMPLE OUTPUTS
---------------
Low risk patient:      0% → flat trajectory
Moderate risk patient: 25% → rising from 27% to 38% over 6 months
High risk patient:     99% → immediate intervention needed
